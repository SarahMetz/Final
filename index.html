Why Visit Japan?

With breathtaking landmarks such as Mt. Fuji, diverse and unique food specialties in every prefecture, and a vibrant anime culture that has influenced the world, Japan offers something for every traveler. The country also treasures its relationship with nature and animals, whether it’s the sacred deer of Nara, snow monkeys bathing in hot springs, or even the famous cat and fox islands.

Beyond culture and scenery, Japan is known for its safety and cleanliness, consistently ranking among the lowest in crime worldwide. Travelers are often impressed by the well-organized public transport, spotless streets, and the welcoming hospitality of the people.

On top of all this, visiting Japan is currently more affordable than ever. With the Japanese yen at one of its lowest exchange rates in history, visitors can experience world-class cuisine, high-tech cities, and traditional heritage sites without stretching their budget.

With so many layers of history, beauty, and modern wonder, Japan is a destination that truly blends the old and the new—making it an unforgettable place to visit.

Would the Language Barrier be a Problem?

Even though Japanese is the main language, the language barrier really isn’t a big problem when visiting Japan. Most train stations, airports, and popular tourist spots have signs and announcements in English, so it’s easy to find your way around. On top of that, translation apps make things even smoother. You can point your camera at menus or signs and instantly know what they say. A lot of restaurants even have picture menus or QR codes with English options. And while not everyone speaks English, people are usually super polite and willing to help, often using gestures or simple words to get the point across.

But you will be amazed how just learning a few phrases can help get you around Japan and Japanese people will appreciate that you tried to learn their language. Learning a couple of basic phrases like “thank you” (ありがとう/arigatou) and “excuse me” (すみません/sumimasen) goes a long way, too. Getting around Japan without speaking the language is way easier than you might expect.
